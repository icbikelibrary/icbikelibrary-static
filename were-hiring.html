1/20/24: Full-time Youth Bike Coordinator
Youth Bike Coordinator PDF
Job Title: Youth Bike Coordinator

Position Overview:
The Bike Library has proudly hosted regularly scheduled youth programs for the past six years, reaching youth most in need of support through close collaboration with partner organizations. We are now seeking a Youth Bike Coordinator to orchestrate our growing programs, manage volunteers, and maintain strong partnerships.The ideal candidate will have a passion for teaching, a strong ability to work with young people, and either a moderate to expert understanding of bike mechanics or a fervent desire to learn. A background in social work or youth programming is a plus.

Job Duties & Responsibilities
Programming and Curriculum Implementation
  • Coordinate and lead youth programs, including:
    • Bike Club for K-6 students; seasonal.
    • Bike Camp, a six-week series of week-long camps; summer.
    • OutSpoken Teens, a weekly earn-a-bike program, year-round.
  • Implement a nationally developed youth bike safety curriculum in all programming.
  • Develop and expand initiatives for specific groups, such as girl-specific programming and neighborhood bike-to-school rides.
Community Engagement and Outreach
  • Conduct outreach to recruit youth participants from schools and local communities, focusing on underserved neighborhoods.
  • Partner with nonprofits, schools, and other organizations to provide resources and programming to youth.
  • Act as a liaison between the Bike Library and partner organizations to ensure clear communication and collaboration.
Instruction and Teaching
  • Teach STEM concepts through bicycle mechanics and riding.
  • Provide instruction on safe cycling practices and bicycle maintenance to youth participants.
  • Create clear and engaging project instructions for participants.
Volunteer Coordination
  • Schedule and manage volunteers for youth programs in collaboration with other staff. 
  • Provide training and development opportunities for volunteers to support youth programming.
Administrative and Fundraising Support
  • Maintain accurate records of youth participants, including parental consent forms.
  • Work closely with the Director and Fundraising Coordinator to market youth programs to potential donors.
  • Provide accurate data and demographics of youth served to support grant applications and donor reporting.
Shop Support
  • Assistance with shop support as needed.
  • Repair bikes for the Checkout System and As-Is Bikes selection in the slow season.
Qualifications
  • Passion for teaching and working with young people.
  • Strong communication and organizational skills.
  • Moderate to expert understanding of bike mechanics or a strong desire to learn.
  • Background in social work, education, or a related field is a plus.
  • Ability to work collaboratively with diverse communities and partner organizations.
  • Commitment to fostering confidence and independence in youth through cycling.

Why This Role Matters
Skills for Life: Hiring a Youth Bike Coordinator helps kids and teens develop essential life skills, learn trades, and build healthy, long-term behaviors that will benefit them for years to come.
Transportation Independence: This role empowers youth with the tools and knowledge to achieve transportation agency, planting the seeds for lifelong bike commuting and sustainable travel habits.
Community Development: The Bike Library will become a vital "third space" where youth can connect, learn, and thrive. This position strengthens the ties between young people and their community, creating a more engaged and resilient local network.

There is an urgent and growing need for youth and teen bike programming, particularly in underserved communities. Expanding our staff allows the Bike Library to meet these needs and provide meaningful programming at every stage of childhood. By investing in youth cycling education and skill-building, we are creating safer cyclists, confident bike mechanics, and a stronger, more connected community.

Work Schedule
  • Full-time salaried: 40 hours per week.
  • The position will include local travel and some evenings and events.
Pay Rate
  • $41,600 - $50,000 depending on experience. 
Benefits
  • Healthcare: 100% of premium.
  • Paid Vacation:
    • 1 week for the 1st year, increasing by 1 week every year to a maximum of 4 weeks.
  • Paid Holidays: 10 per year.
  • Personal Days: 2 per year.
Additional Info: The position requires onsite support with the option to do some administrative work off-site.
Reports To
  • Audrey Wiedemeier, Executive Director
To Apply: Please submit your resume and cover letter to Audrey via email at audrey@bikelibrary.org
Deadline to apply: February 18th. A decision will be made by mid-March pending we have qualified applicants.
