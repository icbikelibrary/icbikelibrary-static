FAQ's

Are you currently accepting donations?
Yes, and Thank you! You can drop donations at 1222 South Gilbert Court ANYTIME DAY OR NIGHT--just use the locks provided on the bike rack out front to lock them up. We are open Wednesdays and Fridays from 12 to 5 pm and Saturdays 10 - 3 if you want to drop them off when someone is here, and we can also give you a tax deductible receipt at this time. 
Are you checking out bikes? 
We are checking out bikes on a first come, first serve basis. We are open for Bicycle Checkout WEDNESDAY & FRIDAY 12 - 5 PM AND SATURDAYS 10 - 3 PM. 

Step 1: Determine if you want a Checkout or an As-is bike. A Checkout Bike is a 6-month rental with the option to buy. For $100 - $300 you get a bike for 6 months. YOU CHOOSE if you want to return the bike for a refund minus $50 OR KEEP THE BIKE and we keep your deposit. We sell As-is bikes too. Those are final sale with no trade-ins or returns. Our higher-end As-is bikes (over $300) are listed on the website; most of the lower-end bikes (~$20-$300) are not listed and you can come in the shop and browse them when we are open. All of the lower-end As-is bikes will need to be fixed in some capacity. 

Step 2: Look at our inventory of Checkout Bikes. If there are none listed then we are currently out of Checkout bikes. Check back again later. Look at our inventory of higher-end As-is bikes if that is an option that interests you.
​
Step 3: Come by 1222 South Gilbert Court Wednesday or Friday from 12 - 5 or Saturdays 10 - 3. 
CAN I BUY AN AS-IS BIKE?
We do have High-end As-Is bikes for sale. We post a few of them here, and regularly on Instagram and Facebook. Most of the lower-end As-is bikes are not listed on the website. You can come look at the As-is bikes in the shop when we are open. 
What is a Checkout Bike?
  • Bike has been refurbished by a staff or volunteer mechanic
  • Bike has been final checked and test ridden by a second staff or volunteer mechanic
  • Bike is READY to ride
  • Bike qualifies for the checkout program (i.e., you have the option to return it within six months for a return on your deposit minus the sustainability fee).
Go to our Checkout Bikes page for more information!

Bikes & Bike Parts
​The Bike Library accepts donations of complete bikes, partial bikes, bike parts and accessories. We prefer bikes in good or repairable condition.

If you have to choose between taking your bike to the dump or bring it to us, please bring it to us! Even bikes that aren't up to our standard for fixing we will strip them down, separate the metal and rubber, and recycle everything we can. It's a free service we offer the community. Our Wednesday night crew of volunteers works hard to keep bikes out of the landfill. 

You can drop off your donations anytime we are open or leave bikes in the bike rack outside and we will get them ASAP.

​Frequently Asked Questions

How do I checkout a bike?
​Checkouts are available during the hours posted on our homepage on a first-come, first-served basis. We do not hold or guarantee a bike for checkout. We recommend that whoever plans to ride the bike is available for a test ride before checkout as sizing is key. During peak time our demand usually exceeds our supply.
Where is the bike library?
​The Bike Library is now located at 1222 S. Gilbert Court; just south all of Iowa City's bike shops.
What payment methods are accepted?
​Cash, Check, or Credit Card
How much does it cost to checkout a bike?
​Our checkout bike deposit runs from $75 to $300, however the majority of our bikes are priced between $100-$200.

If you return the bike within six months, you will be refunded your deposit minus a $50 sustainability fee and any damages requiring replacement parts &/or extensive repair. The sustainability fee helps the Bike Library cover the expenses of normal bicycle wear and the overhead of running our shop.

During the six-month checkout period the Bike Library will assist you with adjustments and repairs, for minimal or no charge, depending on the circumstances. Ask a volunteer or email info@bikelibrary.org if you have questions.

If you keep the bike beyond six months, the Bike Library keeps your deposit, which helps us keep the lights on & the wrenches spinning.
How long do I get the checkout bike?
​The bike is yours for up to six months from date of contract. Your receipt will have a due date listed. If you decide to keep the bike beyond six months, you forfeit your deposit.
How do I return a checkout bike?
Bike returns can be made anytime volunteers are at the shop. As of January 2017, a $50 sustainability fee will be taken from your checkout to cover normal wear and tear of the bicycle and general expenses of running the shop.
​
We ask for an address upon checkout and return of bike. A check will be mailed between 4-6 weeks. Additional charges will be deducted from your deposit if there is damage beyond normal wear and tear. Please ask a volunteer or email info@bikelibrary.org if you need clarification on this policy.
What if I have repair needs on my checkout bike?
​If you have repair needs within the six-month checkout period, stop by with the bicycle, and we will work together to resolve the issue. The Bike Library will cover the cost of minor repairs & adjustments.

For larger repairs we do our best to keep your expense low by using second-hand parts when available and proving labor without charge. Please, be aware that you may need to leave the bike in our shop, depending on availability of volunteer mechanics and any necessary parts.
How do I donate a bike or bike-related items?
​You can donate bike-related items any time volunteers are at the shop. We will give you a receipt for tax purposes for your donation. Alternatively, you can leave bikes outside in the bike rack if we are not open and you need to drop off outside of our hours. 
How do I volunteer?
Stop by to fill out an application. Our volunteer coordinator will contact you with an orientation date. You can email info@bikelibrary.org with additional questions.
Can I bring my bike for repair?
​We are not a commercial bike shop and do not do repairs for-hire. We only service checkout bicycles that are still within the 6-month checkout period. (See above for more about repairs to checkout bikes.)

We offer rent-a-bench (see hours on our main page) for $5 per hour in which you have full access to our tools and repair stands to do your own repairs. Some guidance is available, but we encourage you to do research beforehand on procedures that are new or unfamiliar to you. Used parts can be purchased when available, and some new parts are available to active RAB users.
Do you have kids' bikes?
​Yes, kids' bikes are available for purchase on Saturdays, 10am–3pm. Prices can range from $10 - $50. Kids' bike are not available under our bike checkout program; in other words, you cannot return kids' bikes.

​About

​The Bike Library started in 2004 as a volunteer-run community bike project located in Iowa City, Iowa with a mission of getting more people on bikes. We repair donated bikes and offer them to the public. Bikes are priced with a deposit and checked out for six month periods. If a bike is returned in good condition within the checkout period, the deposit will be returned to the user. Alternately, patrons can choose to keep their bikes and forfeit their deposit.
